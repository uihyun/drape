# drape — Content Production Playbook (v1)

A self-contained brief for producing social content for **drape**. Hand this to a
creator / agency / AI tool as-is. Targets **Instagram (Reels) · Threads · TikTok**
for **🇰🇷 Korea · 🇺🇸 US · 🇯🇵 Japan**.

> Working language: instructions in Korean for the team; deliverable copy
> (on-screen text, captions, prompts) given in EN / KR / JP — use the target
> market's. AI prompts are in English (best results).

---

## 0. How to use this doc
1. Read **§1 Brand** + **§2 Strategy** first — they lock the look/voice.
2. Most content = **real in-app screen recordings** (see §8). AI video (§7) is
   only for brand intros / mood cuts — never to fake the product.
3. Shoot once → **3-language subtitles × 3 platforms** = up to 9 posts per asset.
4. Use the **calendar (§5)** for order; the **scripts (§6)** are ready to film.

---

## 1. Brand snapshot — LOCK THESE

- **Name:** `drape` — **always lowercase**, everywhere (incl. sentence start).
- **What it is:** your wardrobe, on your phone — a **digital closet** (snap a
  garment → background auto-removed + auto-tagged), **AI virtual try-on**
  (put any look on *your real face & body*, any background), an **OOTD calendar**
  (daily outfit diary), a **lookbook feed**, and a **peer marketplace**.
- **The ONE thing (hero hook):** *"See any outfit on the real you, anywhere —
  before you wear or buy it."* The face/body stays unmistakably yours; only the
  clothes (and optionally the scene) change.
- **Voice:** calm, editorial, confident, a little luxe. Lowercase. Short
  sentences. No hype-y exclamation spam. Think *quiet fashion magazine*, not
  *loud app ad*.
- **Palette:** ivory `#F4F1EA` · espresso ink `#141312` · pine accent `#2E4A3A`
  · soft beige neutrals. High contrast, lots of negative space.
- **Type:** the wordmark is **Didot-style italic serif** (ivory on ink). Body /
  captions: clean grotesk (Helvetica/Inter-like). Keep text minimal and elegant.
- **Logo / OG:** ivory italic `drape` on espresso ink. Files in §11.

**Feel reference:** Aesop / The Row / a film-photo OOTD account — restrained,
tactile, expensive-looking. NOT neon, NOT meme-heavy (except where a TikTok
trend calls for it).

## 2. Strategy in one line

**The product *is* the content.** A try-on transformation (inspo look → the real
you wearing it → in any place) is inherently a scroll-stopping before/after. 80%
of content is just *showing real app output beautifully*; AI-generated film is
seasoning.

## 3. Content pillars

| # | Pillar | Why it works | Core proof |
|---|---|---|---|
| ① | **Steal the look** — recreate a feed/influencer outfit on yourself | "will it suit *me*?" curiosity | identity stays yours |

| ④ | **OOTD diary** — a month of looks filling the calendar | aspirational habit | editorial beauty |

①② = hook (novelty). ③④ = "why I'd keep using it" (utility/habit).

## 6. Ready-to-shoot scripts

On-screen text given EN / KR / JP — burn in the target market's. Keep cuts fast.

### Script A — "Steal the look" (D1, hero) · Reel/TikTok 10–12s
- **0.0–1.0s** — open ON THE RESULT (you in the recreated outfit). Text:
  EN `wait… this is AI?` · KR `이거 AI라고?` · JP `これAIなの?`
- **1.0–3.0s** — screen-record: scrolling the feed, tap an influencer's OOTD.
  Text: EN `found this look on my feed` · KR `피드에서 본 이 룩` · JP `フィードで見たこのルック`
- **3.0–6.5s** — tap *Try on* → loading → the result appears (your face/body in
  that outfit). Text: EN `put it on the real me` · KR `진짜 내 모습에 입혀봄` · JP `本当の自分に着せてみた`
- **6.5–9.0s** — slow zoom on the face. Text:
  EN `same face. same body. new outfit.` · KR `얼굴도 체형도 그대로. 옷만 바뀜.` · JP `顔も体型もそのまま。服だけ。`
- **9.0–11s** — end card (ink bg, ivory `drape`). Text:
  EN `drape — try any look on you` · KR `drape — 어떤 룩이든 내 모습으로` · JP `drape — どんなルックも自分の姿で`
- **Sound:** trending soft/satisfying audio; cut the reveal on the beat.
- **CTA caption:** "free on the App Store · link in bio".

### Script B — "Teleport" (D2) · Reel 12–15s
- **0–1s** — result #1 (outfit, Venice). Text: EN `same outfit…` · KR `같은 옷…` · JP `同じ服で…`
- **1–8s** — quick cuts: same outfit, background → beach → city street → café.
  Each ~1.5s, snap on the beat. Text per cut: the place name.
- **8–12s** — grid of all 4. Text: EN `one outfit. anywhere.` · KR `옷 하나로, 어디든.` · JP `一着で、どこへでも。`
- **12–14s** — end card.
- **Note:** generate these in-app via the background field (e.g. "walking in
  Venice", "on a beach at sunset", "Seongsu street"). Use real app outputs.

### Script C — "POV" (D6, TikTok-native) · 8–12s
- **0–1s** — POV text over a paused influencer photo:
  EN `POV: the perfect outfit, but you don't know if it suits you` ·
  KR `POV: 완벽한 옷인데 나한테 어울릴지 모를 때` · JP `POV: 完璧な服、でも似合うか不安`
- **1–6s** — tap → try-on → reveal yourself in it.
- **6–9s** — Text: EN `now i know.` · KR `이제 안다.` · JP `もう分かる。`
- **Sound:** a trending POV/"reveal" audio.

### Script D — "Closet goes digital" (D4, ASMR) · Reel 10s
- **0–1s** — a messy pile of clothes / a single garment photo. Text:
  EN `digitize your closet in seconds` · KR `옷장을 몇 초 만에 디지털로` · JP `クローゼットを数秒でデジタルに`
- **1–7s** — screen-record: photograph a piece → background dissolves away →
  auto-tags appear (category/color). Satisfying, clean SFX (whoosh/pop).
- **7–10s** — the closet grid filling with clean cut-out pieces. Text:
  EN `every piece, tagged & searchable` · KR `모든 옷이 태그되고 검색돼` · JP `すべての服がタグ付け・検索可能`

## 7. AI video (Higgsfield / Kling / Runway) — when & how

**Use AI video ONLY for:** brand intro (3–5s), mood/transition cuts, abstract
texture. **Never** to fake the try-on product (real screen recording is more
credible). Stitch 3–5s AI clips → 15–25s reel.

**Prompt formula:** `[subject] + [action] + [camera move] + [lighting/mood] +
[style/quality] + [setting] + [palette]`

**Brand intro (3–5s):**
> Ivory serif italic word "drape" slowly forming from soft fabric folds on a deep
> espresso-ink background (#141312), slow push-in, soft directional light, matte
> luxurious texture, minimal, editorial, 4k.

**Mood cut — figure in a place (3–5s):**
> A young woman in a beige ribbed knit dress walking slowly along a Venice canal
> alley, slow cinematic dolly-in, golden-hour soft side light, editorial fashion
> film, 35mm, shallow depth of field, muted warm palette, calm and elegant.

**Mood cut — fabric/closet (3–5s):**
> Ivory and beige garments drifting on a soft breeze against espresso-ink
> backdrop, slow orbit, gentle rim light, tactile knit and linen texture,
> minimalist still-life motion, 4k.

**Tips:** always name a **camera move** (push-in / dolly / orbit) and **lighting**
(golden hour / soft side light); add quality words (35mm / editorial / 4k); force
the **palette** (ivory #F4F1EA, ink #141312, beige) for brand consistency.

## 8. Screen-recording capture guide (do this in the app)

Capture clean device recordings (iOS screen record, hide the clock/notch via a
clean status bar if possible). Record these reusable clips:
1. **Feed scroll + tap an OOTD** → the post detail.
2. **Try-on flow:** tap try-on → loading state → result reveal. (Capture a few
   *good* results in advance.)
3. **Background variants:** same look, type different backgrounds → 3–4 results.
4. **Add to closet:** photograph a garment → auto cutout → auto-tags appear.
5. **Calendar:** a month populated with OOTDs (scrub the month).
6. **Result close-ups:** slow pinch-zoom on a result's face (identity proof).

Keep ~30–60s of raw per clip so the editor has room. Prefer real, pretty results
(good lighting source photos → better try-ons).

## 9. Captions & hashtags by market

Keep captions short, lowercase, editorial. End with a soft CTA ("free · link in
bio" / "무료 · 프로필 링크" / "無料・プロフィールから").

- **🇰🇷** tags: `#OOTD #데일리룩 #코디 #코디추천 #AI패션 #가상피팅 #옷장정리 #룩북`
- **🇯🇵** tags: `#コーデ #今日の服 #ootd #AI試着 #ファッション #バーチャル試着 #着回し`
- **🇺🇸** tags: `#ootd #grwm #virtualtryon #fashiontech #thriftflip #aifashion #styleinspo`

(6–10 tags max; mix 2–3 broad + 3–4 niche.)

---

## 10. Do's & don'ts

**Do:** lead with the result; keep it lowercase + editorial; show *real* app
output; emphasize identity preservation (the face/body is really you); subtitle
in-language; cut on the beat.

**Don't:** fake the product with pure AI render and imply it's the app; use real
users' faces without consent; capitalize "Drape"; over-stuff hashtags; loud
hype-y voice; show another person's body as the result (the point is *your* body).
